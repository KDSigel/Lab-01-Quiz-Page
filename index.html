Do you Talk the Rock?

Adam Clark Curry (born September 3, 1964) is a podcaster, announcer, Internet entrepreneur and media personality, known for his stint as VJ on MTV and being one of the first celebrities personally to create and administer Web sites. Also known for co-hosting the No Agenda show, in the 2000s, he first became involved in podcasting, and has been called the 'Podfather' because of his efforts.

Curry was born in Arlington, Virginia, but lived in Amstelveen, Netherlands, from 1972 to 1987. After a time working in Dutch pirate radio at Radio Picasso in Amstelveen and Radio Decibel [nl] in Amsterdam in the early 1980s under the pseudonym "John Holden"

  • In 1987, Curry became a VJ for MTV.
  • He was also host of the programs Headbangers Ball and MTV Top 20 Video Countdown.
  • Curry registered the then-unclaimed domain name "mtv.com" in 1993 with the idea of being MTV's unofficial new voice on the Internet.
  • MTV subsequently sued him for the domain name.
  • n 2002 he produced and starred in the reality soap Adam's Family: een kijkje in het leven van de familie Curry
  • Curry is the nephew of former CIA official and United States Ambassador to Korea, Donald Gregg, whom he calls "Uncle Don" in his podcast.

Podcasting

  1. Curry founded PodShow with his business partner Ron Bloom, in January 2005.
  2. From June 2005 to May 2007, Curry hosted a weekday evening show called 'Adam Curry's PodShow'.
  3. Since October 2007, he has hosted the twice-weekly podcast the No Agenda Show with John C. Dvorak.
